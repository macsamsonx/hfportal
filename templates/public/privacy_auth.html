Data Privacy Policy

Hundredfold Digital Marketing — Pursuant to Republic Act No. 10173

🖨️ Print

Last updated: January 2026  ·  Effective date: January 1, 2026

1. Introduction

Hundredfold Digital Marketing ("the Company," "we," "us," or "our") is committed to protecting the privacy and security of your personal data. This Data Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, store, and protect your personal information in accordance with Republic Act No. 10173, also known as the Data Privacy Act of 2012 (DPA), its Implementing Rules and Regulations (IRR), and the issuances of the National Privacy Commission (NPC) of the Philippines.

2. Data Controller Information

The Company acts as the Personal Information Controller (PIC) in relation to the personal data collected through this Employee Portal. Our designated Data Protection Officer (DPO) may be contacted through the HR Manager at our official office address or by sending an email to the company's official address.

3. Scope

This policy applies to all employees, applicants, and contractors whose personal data is processed through the Hundredfold Employee Portal, including registration, attendance tracking, payroll, leave management, and related employment activities.

4. Personal Data We Collect

We collect and process the following categories of personal data:

  • Identity data: Full name (prefix, first, middle, last name, nickname), gender, date of birth, photograph
  • Contact data: Email address, phone number, home address
  • Employment data: Position applied for, skills and capabilities, employment type, department, shift schedule, employment start and end dates
  • Financial data: Hourly rate, payroll records, deduction enrollment (SSS, PhilHealth, Pag-IBIG, BIR/TIN)
  • Attendance data: Clock-in/clock-out times, leave requests, overtime records, time cards
  • Government IDs: SSS number, PhilHealth number, Pag-IBIG number, TIN — collected only for government-mandated contributions and remittances
  • Emergency contact data: Name and contact number of designated emergency contact person

5. Purposes and Legal Bases of Processing

We process your personal data based on the following lawful bases under Section 12 and 13 of the Data Privacy Act:

  • Consent (Sec. 12[a]): Processing of data provided during registration and onboarding, based on your express consent as given through this portal.
  • Contractual necessity (Sec. 12[b]): Processing necessary for the performance of your employment contract, including payroll computation, attendance monitoring, and leave administration.
  • Legal obligation (Sec. 12[c]): Processing required by applicable Philippine law, including the Labor Code of the Philippines (P.D. 442), DOLE Regulations, SSS Act (R.A. 11199), PhilHealth Act (R.A. 7875 as amended by R.A. 10606), HDMF Law (R.A. 9679), and the National Internal Revenue Code (NIRC).
  • Legitimate interests (Sec. 12[f]): Processing necessary for legitimate business operations including performance management, internal communications, and organizational planning, provided such interests are not overridden by your fundamental rights and freedoms.

6. Data Retention

We retain your personal data only for as long as necessary for the purposes described in this policy and as required by applicable Philippine law:

  • Employment records: retained for a minimum of 10 years from termination of employment, in accordance with BIR and DOLE requirements
  • Payroll records and tax documents: retained in accordance with the NIRC, minimum 10 years
  • Pending registration applications not approved: retained for 1 year, then securely deleted
  • Attendance and leave records: retained for 3 years minimum or as required by applicable regulations

7. Data Sharing and Disclosure

We do not sell, trade, or rent your personal data to third parties. Your data may be shared with:

  • Government agencies as required by law: Social Security System (SSS), Philippine Health Insurance Corporation (PhilHealth), Home Development Mutual Fund (Pag-IBIG/HDMF), Bureau of Internal Revenue (BIR), and Department of Labor and Employment (DOLE)
  • Authorized personnel within the Company on a need-to-know basis: HR Managers, Administrators, and relevant supervisors
  • Service providers engaged by the Company who process data on our behalf and are bound by appropriate data processing agreements

Any cross-border transfers of personal data, if applicable, shall be conducted in accordance with NPC guidelines on data sharing and cross-border data flows.

8. Your Rights as a Data Subject

Under the Data Privacy Act of 2012 (Sections 16–18), you have the following rights:

  • Right to be informed — to be informed of the processing of your personal data
  • Right of access — to access your personal data held by the Company
  • Right to rectification — to correct inaccurate or incomplete personal data
  • Right to erasure/blocking — to have your data removed or blocked, subject to legal retention requirements
  • Right to object — to object to the processing of your personal data in certain circumstances
  • Right to data portability — to obtain a copy of your data in a structured, machine-readable format
  • Right to file a complaint — to lodge a complaint with the National Privacy Commission (NPC)
  • Right to damages — to claim compensation for damages sustained due to inaccurate, incomplete, or unauthorized processing

To exercise any of these rights, please contact the HR Manager or the designated Data Protection Officer in writing. We will respond to your request within 15 working days from receipt.

9. Security Measures

The Company implements appropriate technical and organizational security measures to protect your personal data against unauthorized access, use, disclosure, alteration, loss, or destruction. These measures include password hashing (bcrypt), access controls, role-based permissions within the Employee Portal, and secure storage of data on-premise. We conduct periodic reviews of our security practices consistent with the requirements of the National Privacy Commission.

10. Data Breach Protocol

In the event of a personal data breach that may result in harm to data subjects, the Company shall notify the National Privacy Commission and affected data subjects within 72 hours of becoming aware of the breach, in accordance with NPC Circular 16-03 and its amendments.

11. Withdrawal of Consent

You may withdraw consent at any time by submitting a written request to the HR Manager. Withdrawal of consent does not affect the lawfulness of processing carried out prior to withdrawal, and does not affect processing carried out on other legal bases (e.g., legal obligation or contractual necessity).

12. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Data Privacy Policy periodically to reflect changes in law, technology, or our business practices. Any significant changes will be communicated to employees through the portal or by direct notice.

13. How to Contact Us / File a Complaint

For privacy-related inquiries or to exercise your data subject rights, contact our HR Manager or Data Protection Officer through the Company's official channels.

To file a complaint with the regulatory authority:

  • National Privacy Commission
  • Email: complaints@privacy.gov.ph
  • Website: www.privacy.gov.ph
  • Address: 5th Floor, Delegation Building, PICC Complex, Pasay City, Metro Manila
